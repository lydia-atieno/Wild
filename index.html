Interesting facts about 'The Big Five'.

Written by Lydia Gawala

1. The Lion

In Swahili, a lion is called Simba.
The scientific name for a lion is Panthera leo.
They are the second largest big cats after tigers.
There are two surviving species of lions: African lions and Asian lions.
The young one of a lion is called a cub.
A group of lions is known as a pride. Tanzania has the largest population of lions in Africa.
  • Although the lion is known as "the king of the jungle", lions do not live in jungles. They live only in grasslands and plains.
  • Lions come in different colors, including tan, brown, yellow and even red.
  • In the wild, male lions live for 12-16years, lionesses live for about 15-18years. In captivity, male lions can live for over 20years.
  • Only about 1 in 8 male lions survive to adulthood. A majority of lions die shortly after being kicked out of their pride around the age of 2.
  • A lion's roar can be heard up to eight kilometres away.
  • Unlike other cats, lions are great swimmers.
  • They have a great sense of hearing.
  • The lion is the most social of all big cats.
  • Among all the big cats, lions nap the most, sleeping up to 22hours a day.
  • Lion prides are usually decribed as matriarchal, with communal care for the young. Most lionesses in a pride are related and remain in the same pride for life.Only two or three lions within a pride will be males, who are either brothers or pride-mates.

  • A dominant male in a pride has two jobs. First, he must mate with all the females in the pride and second, he must defend the pride from other males who want to take over.
  • Male lions may mate up to 100 times in two days to ensure that the female is pregnant. Each mating lasts only a few seconds. Female lions are pregnamt with their cubs for about three and a half months.
  • The lion has a special tongue that is rough enough to peel the skin of their prey away from the flesh. If a lion licked the back of a human's hand a few times, it would peel away much of the skin.
  • A lion's pupil is three times as big as human's
  • A lion can't move its eyes side to side very well, so it moves its entire head when it needs to look in a different direction.
  • Lions mostly hunt at night.
  • Lions can see six times better in the dark than a human.
  • Lions are not as fast as other big cats, they hunt using teamwork and rely more on strength than speed to catch and kill prey.
  • Lionesses will often bring back a small animal such as a baby antelope alive so that the cub can practice their hunting skills.

The Elephant

There are two species of elephants: the African elephant and the Asian elephant.
The scientific name for elephants is Loxodonta.
In Swahili, an elephant is known as Ndovu.
A baby elephant is called a calf.
A group of elephants is called a memory.
  • They are the world's largest land animal.
  • Their tunks are actually teeth. Elephant tunks are actually enlarged incisor teeth which first appear when elephants are around 2 years old. Tunks continue growing throughout their lives.
  • Elephants are constantly eating. They need upto 150kg of food per day. They eat so much that they spend up to three-quaters of their day eating.
  • Elephants communicate through vibrations.
  • An elephant never forgets. It has the temporal lobe (area of the brain associated with memory) that is larger and denser than that of people.
  • Elephants are born blind.
  • Elephants have hair all over their bodies.
  • Elephant family groups are very close.
  • Their skin is about one inch thick.
  • They have eyelashes.
  • The elephant trunk serves as a nose, a hand, an extra foot, a signaling device and a tool for gathering food, siphoning water, dusting, digging and much more.
  • Elephants can swim.
  • It is the only animal that can't jump.
  • The elephants gestation period is 22months.
  • They can hear through their feet.
  • African elephants possess human qualities. They grieve the dead and hold grudges.
  • Elephants support those in need. They coordinate with each other to help a sick, injured or a distressed individual.
  • They can suffer Post-traumatic stress disorder(PTSD). Elephants who experience tragedy, like witnessing a family member being killed by poachers will show PTSD symptoms.
  • Elephants honor the dead. Long after an animal has died, elephants will return and touch the remaining bones with their feet and trunks.

The Leopard

Leopards are part of the big cat family Felidae.
The scientiic name for leopard is Panthera pardus.
The young one of a leopard is called a cub.
A group of leopards is called a leap.
  • The spots on leopards' skin are called rosettes because their shape is similar to that of a rose.
  • Leopards are shy and cunning.
  • Leopards are fast and can run up to 58km/hr.
  • Leopards are solitary and spend most of their time alone.
  • They each have their own territory. They leave scratches on trees, urine scent marks and poop to warn other leopards to stay away.
  • They are skilled climbers and like to rest in the branches of trees during the day.
  • Female leopards have a gestation period of three months and can give birth to two or three cubs.
  • Female leopards will leave her cubs in secret hideways before she goes off to hunt for food.
  • Leopard cubs are born blind and are incredibly vulnerable. Their eyes begin to open around ten days old.
  • Leopards are active at night. They mostly spend their days resting.
  • A leopard's behavior is secretive and territorial.
  • They jump to super heights and are great athletes.

Black Rhinoceros.

There are black rhinos and white rhinos.
The black rhino is the one that belongs to the big five. Rhino species include: the Indian rhino, White rhino, Black rhino, Javan rhino and the Sumatran rhino.
The scientific name for rhinoceros is Rhinocerotidae.The collective name for rhinos is called a crash. They are the second largest animals after elephants.
A baby rhino is called a calf.
  • Black rhinos have two horns.
  • All rhino species can grow to weigh over 1000kg.
  • Rhinos have poor vision. They mainly rely on their strong sense of smell.
  • Despite looking bulky and awkward, a rhino can run really fast. They run a speed of 55km/hr.
  • If a rhino's horn falls off, another one grows in its place.
  • Rhinos love to eat plants and vegetables.
  • Most rhionos' natural deaths occur from fighting. Rhinos are territorial and when males challenge each other, things can get very aggressive and violent.
  • Rhinos are loners. They like to be on their own minding their own business.
  • They use their horns to scratch and dig for food.
  • Black rhinos live up to 40years on average.
  • They love rolling around in mud.
  • Rhinos have a gestation period of between 16 to 18months.
  • Rhinos like to socialise.

The Buffalo

The scientific name for a buffalo is Syncerus caffer.
A group of buffaloes is called a herd, gang, troop or an obstinancy.
A baby buffalo is called a calf.
Buffaloes live for a span of 11 to 22years in the wild and an average of 29years in captivity.
There are four subspecies of the African buffalo: the Cape buffalo, the forest buffalo, the West Africa savanna buffalo and the Central Africa savanna buffalo.
  • Buffaloes are known to kill lions.
  • They are one of the most dangerous of the big five because they simply ambush their prey without giving any type of warning signal.
  • Just like rhinos, buffaloes do not have a very good eyesight but their hearing and especially their smell is exceptional.
  • Buffaloes never forget and never forgive. Wounded buffaloes will ambush hunters that have attacked them in the past.
  • Buffalo horns can distinguish age and sex. Female horns are narrower and smaller.
  • They are excellent swimmers.
  • Buffaloes are grazers and prefer tall grasses.
  • They need to drink about 40 litres of water per day to keep their digestive system operating.
  • Being large animals they can easily overheat, so they feed mostly during the night and rest during the day.
  • They can run for a speed of 57km/hr.
  • If one individual is under attack from a predator, the herd will rush to the victim's defence.
  • Buffaloes mate and give birth only during the rainy season.
  • The gestation period for female buffaloes is 11 months.
